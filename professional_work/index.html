Role
Full Stack Web Developer

I have professional experience as a Full Stack Web Developer and a M.S. in Computer Science. All of the web applications showcased here are solo projects from my role as an Application Developer at Boston University.

During my role as an Application Developer, I was not part of a software team, but as someone who is no stranger to taking the initiative, I would always ask my coworkers for feedback and to test anything I was building.

Understanding the importance of collaboration and drawing from my agile development experience from my graduate program, I created my own agile team and environment. I set up sprints for myself and my own kanban boards, and organized meetings with coworkers to discuss designs, features, and bugs.

Goals
Learn, Improve, Grow

While designing and building these projects, I learned as much as possible about developing on a LAMP stack. I grew as a software engineer as I ventured beyond developing mostly with Java and C++.

I gained experience creating mobile-first web applications and learned how to build and use databases with PostgreSQL and MySQL. Using and integrating various REST APIs into my web apps, I was able to improve my understanding of RESTful web service development.

In any role I have, I always push myself to learn new frameworks, APIs, and languages. I know that growing and improvement never stops, especially in such a fast-paced industry.

Impact
Fresh Perspectives

For any work I do, professional or personal, I always incorporate other people's perspectives. Why? Because I understand that diverse perspectives and feedback lead to innovation and increased market share.

Fresh perspectives lead to new designs and approaches to understanding the ever-changing market. And without innovation, I know that usability and design fall behind user expectations.

Everywhere I go, I think about the design of what is around me. Whether it is intuitive, elegant, or thoughtful. Good or bad design. Design that attracts people or design that frustrates and pushes people away. I understand that building something isn't just prose, it's poetry.
